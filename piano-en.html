About the Piano

When I started playing the piano, the first song I learnt was Mozart's Turkish March. As it was the first time I'd played the piano, I found it very difficult. However, I soon understood how the instrument worked thanks to my grandmother, who was a piano teacher. By watching her play, I was able to memorise the placement of my hands and began to learn my first pieces of music. Classical music gave me a great grounding in piano and enabled me to make a quantum leap towards much more technical songs.

After classical music, I discovered a music arranger called "Animenz" who arranges anime music using a lot of classical techniques and sometimes jazz. I was really impressed by his level, which was beyond anything I'd imagined. So I started to learn his most famous piece 'Unravel', which is probably the most difficult piano piece I've ever learned.

How i Learn

Since I didn't study music at a conservatory like most piano enthusiasts, I had to learn to read music without music theory. Fortunately, there are a lot of videos on youtube that scroll through the notes so that you can visualise the songs, they're called "Synthesia".

For example, here's how I learnt Animenz's Unravel Piece from a Synthesia video. I usually prefer videos where you can't see the hands because I have my own droigt for the notes. The main key points when learning a piece are:

- Memorise the Tempo
- Start with the right hand at reduced speed
- Add the left hand when you have mastered the right hand perfectly
- Play slowly and accelerate when you feel comfortable
